Steve Jobs History

Apple was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak. Steve and Wozniak had a vision of changing the way people view computers, they wanted to make computers small enough for to have them in their homes or offices.

Steve and Wozniak started building the Apple I in Job's garage and sold them without a monitor, keyboard, or casing. The Apple II revolutionized the computer industry wiht the introduction of the first ever color graphics. In 1980 Apple went public.

Steve Wozniak left apple in 1983 due to a lack of interest in the day to day running of Apple Computers. Jobs then hired Pepsi exectutive John Sculley which backfired on him and led to Jobs getting fired in 1985.

After Apple's sales peak in 1990, Apple's market share started to suffer and the company was expected to be doomed. It was not until 1997, when Apple was desperately need of an operating system and they bought out NeXT Software (Steve Jobs' Company). Steve Jobs became interim CEO (he was officially CEO in 2000). In Apple, Steve revamped the computers and introduced the iBook followed by the iPod, which became market leader.

2007 was the year the phone was revolutionized. The iPhone was introduced to the world, a touch screen cellular device that can take pictures, download apps, send messages, and more. Since 2007, Apple have made a new iPhone every single year. In 2018, Apple was the first valued company to reach one trillion dollars and in two year it doubled that figure.
